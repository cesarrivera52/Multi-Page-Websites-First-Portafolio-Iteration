Operations / Environmental Sustainability / External Logistics

San Jose Trouts

September - November 2023

Situation

As the problem situation for this block, we were assigned to work with Truchas San José, a company dedicated to the farming and commercialization of trout. This company has been operating for 30 years. Our objective is to identify areas of improvement in their strategies and various business processes.

Task

To identify the areas for improvement, we decided to carry out a primary data collection process consisting of conducting interviews.

An interview is a conversation between the interviewer and the interviewee. This technique is used to gather information on various topics. The way the interviewer collects information depends on several factors, such as psychological aspects, events that occur during the interview, or even the overall flow of the conversation. It is important to ensure that the questions posed to the interviewee are open-ended rather than closed. This allows the interviewee to provide more detailed and comprehensive responses, which can lead to a deeper understanding of the topic being discussed.

The company follows a clear strategy, as the strategy it pursues is differentiation for the following reasons:

  • The products sold in the restaurant are fresh
  • They do not have many utilities, such as electricity service, so they generate their own electricity through solar panels
  • They have a truck responsible for delivering the sales they make
  • They have 3 operators in charge of carrying out day-to-day activities
  • The concept of their restaurant is an austere one, providing the customer with the experience of enjoying a day in the countryside
  • Their spoilage percentage is less than 8%. Since the business focuses on trout farming, if a trout’s growth stagnates, it can continue in the fattening process until it reaches the desired size. The only way it is considered spoilage is if one dies during the process

Action

Once we had identified the gap we wanted to address, we determined that the main problem to tackle was reducing trout mortality. As a first step, we decided to optimize the data collection of trout deaths, since Truchas San José's owner did not have a standardized way to collect this information. We created an Excel file with clear rows and columns where he could fill in the data to maintain control over this issue.

Having data on the behavior of the ponds makes it easier to identify factors causing trout deaths and to address the problem more effectively.

Results
